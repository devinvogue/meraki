Who We Are

The Mafubo International Federation fights extreme poverty, insecurity and the various forms of violence against women in Africa and elsewhere. Mafubo aims to ensure that girls from disadvantaged backgrounds are guaranteed conditions for academic success. Established in more than 40 countries women under the same banner carrying the same dreams to achieve the same goals. This is what we are.

From the Rwandan culture, the word Mafubo has become a name with an international destiny; Easy to write, read and pronounce, Mafubo means the confidante with a global connotation to designate solidarity between women. Mafubo designates a woman who has a great deal of compassion for the other; she is the one who forgets herself to support and especially to spare the other, shame, humiliation and degradation. Mafubo designates a friend to whom one confides without net and without restraint, especially when one needs to be supported. It is a strong expression of womenâ€™s solidarity, one of the main principles of our Federation. Every woman should be a Mafubo for another woman, and every woman should have a Mafubo in her life. But the important thing is that every woman should deserve another woman to call her Mafubo. This chain of solidarity must take root around us in our communities and then stain and go further. This is how we will build active and borderless solidarity. With the new communication technologies, we have no more excuses. We can do it.



Mission

We prevents and alleviates human suffering in the face of emergencies by mobilizing the power of volunteers and donors. We are is fighting every minute of every day to finish what we started and achieve our vision to advance world understanding, goodwill, and peace through the improvement of health, the support of education, and the alleviation of poverty.

President of Mafubo Ghana

She joined the network of Mafubo Women in November 2015 as the coordinator for Ghana. Prior to this and passionate about empowering people, Linda worked in Ghana with the Touched base International as a Programme Manager and with Vision Masters Incorporated as Data and a Project Officer. Before then, Linda lived in the United Kingdom where she studied and worked. After 8 years experience in active management and business sector in the United Kingdom, she joined the social services as a shadow social worker, a team lead and a project lead with the Brooklands Hospital, Winborne Mental Home and Business Post respectively. She just completed an MBA in Human Resource Management at the Valley View University here in Ghana and has a first degree in Business Management from the Graduate School of Management Studies London Uk. She holds among others, a Diploma in Children Care and Learning Development, from the Bournville College Birmingham UK; a Diploma in Counselling from the Oxford College UK; a certificate in Project Management and a Post Graduate Certificate in Human Resource and Strategic Management, a Diploma in Chill Care Learning and development. Linda is very passionate about helping and empowering others especially women. She is wife, a mother and a career woman with GREAT LOVE OF THE POOR AND NEEDY.
