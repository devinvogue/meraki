From the Rwandan culture, the word Mafubo has become a name with an international destiny; Easy to write, read and pronounce, Mafubo means the confidante with a global connotation to designate solidarity between women. Mafubo designates a woman who has a great deal of compassion for the other; she is the one who forgets herself to support and especially to spare the other, shame, humiliation and degradation. Mafubo designates a friend to whom one confides without net and without restraint, especially when one needs to be supported. It is a strong expression of womenâ€™s solidarity, one of the main principles of our Federation.

Mafubo fights against women's extreme poverty and insecurity. It is apolitical and non-denominational. Projects stem from the needs and priorities identified by local teams.and are implemented with local people. Mafubo is organized so that the impact of its work within a community can be quickly and easily assessed and evaluated. Its effect on communities must be fast and visible.

The Mafubo International Federation fights extreme poverty, insecurity and the various forms of violence against women in Africa and elsewhere. It aims at ensuring that girls from disadvantaged backgrounds are guaranteed conditions for academic success. Mafubo is trying to resolve this is by improving access to quality gender data. We recognize that better gender data will allow us to examine, identify and understand many problems and aspirations specific to women, including maternal mortality rates. The collection and use of this data is central to helping donors better target their spending, while equally enabling civil society to develop more precise recommendations.

It has therefore been established in more than 40 countries where women under the same banner share the same dreams to achieve the same goals.

Founder of Mafubo

Mafubo is a federation of 36 country associations and was created in 2009 by Monique Mujawamariya.It is aimed at helping African women emerge from extreme poverty, insecurity and violence. Mafubo International's activities in 14 countries include access to decent housing, combating domestic violence and other abuses against women in urban areas, access to appropriate care against illnesses Reproductive rights of women and equal opportunities for girls through education.
